History Rwandans at University of Nebraska Lincoln

The Rwandan community at the University of Nebraska Lincoln is on of the majority of international students here on campus. They are pursuing a bachelor's degree in Integrated Science. Integrated Science was designed to help the student to have an idea of what is sustainable agriculture and apply leadership and Entrepreneurship in their communities. The first Cohort came in the fall semester of 2015. It was not an easy way for them to cooperate with the life on campus. During that time they came they had to find out how to live in the new and unusual life on campus.

this cohort graduated in the May 2019 as the class of 2019. It was a great graduation ceremony where some of their parents attended. This one of the achievements of the CUSP committee to see their first cohort graduating.

After 2015 until 2019 there were other four cohorts which came after. Each cohort was made of 50 students. In total the program brought to The University of Nebraska Lincoln 205 students. This number would not get if there was no partnership between the Government of Rwanda and the donnor of the Program

Life of Rwandans here at On Campus

the life of Rwandan students here at the University of Nebraska Lincoln is not different from other international students.
The CUSP scholars are more involved in different activities here on campus. For example the RSOs and also other organizations. On the Other hand, as the culture of many Rwandans, most of students come from Christian backgrounds, thus when they came here in Nebraska they continued to go to Pray and involve in Religious activities here on campus.
